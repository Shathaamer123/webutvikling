Meat-free canteen food at OsloMet

Oslo City Council is committed to halving meat consumption in the municipality's canteens and institutions. OsloMet focuses on vegetarian options but currently has no plans for a meat-free day of the week. Recently, "meat-free Monday" has become popular in several canteens. Now that meat consumption is to be halved, according to the new political platform of the Oslo city council, canteens will eventually have to cut back on meat.

The canteen offer can be improved

The cafeteria offerings could be improved at OsloMet. With a total of 48 bachelor's programs, 38 master's programs, and 7 Ph.D. programs, the university accommodates a student population of 20,635. Journalen decided to visit two different cafeterias in Pilestredet to speak with students and staff about the offerings available. This exploration is in response to VG's research, aiming to determine whether the cafeteria offerings are both good and healthy for budget-conscious students.
